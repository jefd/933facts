Cash Balance Formula for your Pension Plan Benefit

You are receiving this information because you are part of a group of heritage Raytheon employees who participate in a defined benefit pension plan. In January, we wrote to inform you that Raytheon Technologies has decided to change the current defined benefit pension formula to an approach more aligned with our peers, while still providing employees with a source of retirement income that is valuable and flexible.

This change to your pension plan formula does not take effect until Jan. 1, 2023.

In January, we sent out a communication announcing the plan change and effective date. In that announcement, we indicated that we would be following up in Q2 with the cash balance formula that will be used to calculate your benefits beginning in January 2023. This material includes that cash balance formula. You can find a copy of the prior communication on Desktop Benefits under the My Resources tab on the home page.

What is Cash Balance?

Cash balance is a type of defined benefit pension formula that provides a guaranteed pension, but is different from what you have today. Using the cash balance formula, your pension benefit will grow with two types of credits from Raytheon Technologies: pay credits and interest credits. Each month, these credits will be allocated to a cash balance account tracked by the company on your behalf. The pay credits and interest credits you will earn after Dec. 31, 2022, are included below.

There are several benefits of cash balance:

  • You may have more options on how to take your benefit compared with traditional defined benefit formulas (such as the full balance at one-time or a monthly annuity).
  • Your cash balance account is portable, meaning you can take it to another employer or roll over the balance to an IRA if you leave Raytheon Technologies.
  • You can watch your benefits grow and know your balance each month.

It’s also important to note that while cash balance is flexible and simple, if you receive the benefit as a fixed monthly annuity for your lifetime, it may not provide as much monthly income as a traditional pension plan would have in the same circumstances.

Pay Credits

Pay credits are:

  • Determined each year based on the age you will be as of December 31 of that year.
  • Expressed as a percentage of your total compensation (base pay plus annual incentive bonuses that are part of a bonus plan; special one-time bonus payments or awards are excluded).
  • Allocated to your cash balance account at the end of each month.
  • Provided by Raytheon Technologies - they are not deducted from your pay.

Interest Credits

In addition to pay credits, your cash balance account grows with interest credits. The cash balance interest credit rate is tied to the 30-year U.S. Treasury bond average yield for November of the prior calendar year, but with a guaranteed minimum of 3.5%.

Note: Non-qualified Excess Pension Plan

The change to the defined benefit pension plan will also affect the non-qualified excess (or parity) pension plan. If your pay exceeds the IRS pay limit for qualified pension plans, you will still get your pay credits and associated interest credits on the amount of pay that is in excess of the limit in the non-qualified excess plan. We will provide more information about this plan before the change is effective in 2023.

New Raytheon Technologies Savings Plan - coming 2023

When it comes to retirement benefits, our goal is to provide all employees with a source of retirement income that is valuable and flexible. To be consistent across both heritage organizations, beginning in January 2023, you will receive company contributions to the Savings Plan in the form of matching contributions and retirement contributions that are based on age. (Retirement contributions replace the Retirement Income Savings Program (RISP) for eligible employees.) Employees will be vested in (or own) both the company matching and retirement contributions after being employed for two years counting all prior recognized service with heritage Raytheon.

Company Retirement Contributions Effective Jan. 1, 2023

Each pay period, you will receive a company retirement contribution under the Savings Plan expressed as a percentage of your pay. The retirement contribution in each year will be based on the age you will be as of December 31 of that year. As you get older (and closer to retirement), the contribution percentage increases.

Non-Qualified Deferred Compensation

The changes to the company match and retirement contributions will also affect the non-qualified deferred compensation programs. If the amount of match and/or company retirement contributions you otherwise would receive after the changes are effective is limited because of IRS mandated maximums in the savings plan, the excess match and company retirement contributions will go into a non-qualified deferred compensation account. We will provide more information about non-qualified deferred compensation, including how to enroll, before the changes become effective.
